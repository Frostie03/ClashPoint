About Us

Who We Are

Clash Point is a youth-centered educational platform built for high school students who want to think sharper, speak bolder, and engage with the world beyond textbooks. The platform is dedicated to empowering high school students to think sharper, speak bolder, and lead smarter. We provide resources and mentorship in debate, economics, politics, and social systems to help students develop critical skills for the future.

Our Mission

To empower students through debate, economics, politics, and social analysis - shaping them into the leaders and thinkers of tomorrow.

Debate Resources

Everything you need to level up your debate game. From motions and strategy guides to step-by-step training tips - practical tools that help you think faster, argue smarter, and speak with confidence.

Debate & Public Speaking Resources:

  • ESU - free speech & debate resources, activities, lesson plans
  • My Debate Resource - free resources for students, coaches, etc.
  • Debatabase (by IDEA) - over 700 debate topics with arguments on both sides.
  • National Speech & Debate Association - resources for students, coaches, rounds, etc.
  • Debate Resource - sample cases, strategy, how-to research tips.
  • Kialo Edu - helps map arguments, visualize how ideas link together.

Economic & Politics

Our Economic & Politics resources simplify complex topics, making them accessible and engaging for students. We provide simulations, exam prep materials, and expert insights to help students navigate these critical subjects.

Economics & Political Literacy Resources:

  • Federal Reserve Education (FRE.org) - economics & personal finance resources
  • Econ Lowdown - free economics / finance tools & teaching modules.
  • AEA “Resources for Students” - economics overview, student tools
  • EconLib - economics & liberty resources for students & teachers
  • Economics resources - from University of Illinois / other academic collections

Mentorship Hub

Our Mentorship Hub connects students with online resources, notes, Q&As, mock debates, and discussion forums.

Social Analysis / Politics / Current Affairs Resources:

  • E-International Relations (E-IR) - international politics, essays, analysis
  • Open to Debate - nonpartisan debate discussions on current issues
  • Research guides - listing Pro & Con, opposing viewpoints, etc.
